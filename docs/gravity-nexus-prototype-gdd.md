# **Project Overview**

## **Purpose**

This prototype isolates the Gravity Nexus crew system in a simplified text-adventure format. The goal is to design, test, and iterate on crew mechanics — recruitment, morale, relationships, species dynamics, history, and deep narrative systems — without the complexity of 3D rendering, ship physics, or real-time combat. If the crew system generates interesting emergent stories in a text-only context, it will work in the full game.

## **Scope**

A complete playable game with a 2–3 hour playthrough. The player progresses through three ship classes (solo, small crew, full crew), running missions, trading goods, and managing a crew across a 12-planet node graph. All five tiers of the crew system GDD are implemented. The game is won by reaching a defined credit target.

## **Technology**

**Engine:** Godot 4.5+ with GDScript.

**Database:** SQLite for persistent game state, crew data, universe state, and save/load.

**Art:** Placeholder geometry with shader support. Planet visuals use an existing planet shader applied to square panels. UI uses Godot’s built-in theming with a custom dark sci-fi palette.

**Narrative:** Atmospheric text snippets using templated prose with procedural fill-in (crew names, species, locations). Stored in SQLite or resource files.

## **Design Pillars**

**Reactive, not interactive.** The player expresses themselves through recruitment, risk tolerance, and mission choice. The crew responds. No micromanagement.

**Emergent narrative.** Crew events, relationships, and history are generated by interlocking systems, not scripted stories.

**Every composition is valid.** No role restrictions. Weird crews are viable but lopsided. Consequences come through natural feedback.

**Development testbed.** Debug tools allow isolated testing of any tier or system. This is a prototype, not a product.

## **Target Playthrough Pacing**

| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |

# **Core Systems**

## **Ships**

| Class | Name | Crew Slots | Cargo Hold | Fuel Tank | Hull HP | Unlock Level | Approximate Price |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Starter | Skiff | 0 (solo) | 10 units | 30 | 50 | 1 (start) | Free (you begin with it) |
| Medium | Corvette | 3 | 25 units | 60 | 120 | 4 | \~2,000 credits |
| Large | Frigate | 12 | 50 units | 100 | 250 | 7 | \~8,000 credits |

Fuel consumption scales with ship class. Larger ships burn more fuel per jump but carry more, so efficient route planning matters. When the player buys a new ship, existing crew transfer automatically.

## **Captain**

The captain is a human character with simplified stats functioning as a generalist. All five stats start at 45 on a 0–100 scale. The captain gains \+2 to all stats per level, reaching 63 at level 10\. The captain covers any unfilled role at this generalist effectiveness, and can only perform one function at a time (the core crew system premise).

| Stat | Starting Value | Level 10 Value | Function |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Stamina | 45 | 63 | Physical tasks, endurance under pressure |
| Cognition | 45 | 63 | Technical tasks, navigation, science |
| Reflexes | 45 | 63 | Combat responsiveness, evasion |
| Social | 45 | 63 | Diplomacy, negotiation, crew interaction |
| Resourcefulness | 45 | 63 | Improvisation, efficiency, gap-filling |

| Level | XP Required (Cumulative) | Unlocks |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | 0 | Game start. Skiff-class ship. |
| 2 | 100 | No unlock. Progression milestone. |
| 3 | 250 | No unlock. |
| 4 | 500 | Corvette-class purchase eligible. |
| 5 | 800 | No unlock. |
| 6 | 1,200 | No unlock. |
| 7 | 1,800 | Frigate-class purchase eligible. |
| 8 | 2,500 | No unlock. |
| 9 | 3,500 | No unlock. |
| 10 | 5,000 | Max level. |

XP comes from: completing missions (base XP \+ difficulty bonus), surviving encounters (small XP), and trading (very small XP per profitable trade). Harder missions and dangerous encounters award more.

## **Economy & Trading**

### **Six Trade Commodities**

| Good | Description | Cheap At | Expensive At |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Food | Crew sustenance. Also a trade good. | Fallow, Lirien | Dvarn, Korrath Prime, Char |
| Fuel | Ship fuel. Also tradeable in bulk. | Windhollow, Dvarn | Tessara, Nexus Station |
| Ore | Raw extracted minerals. | Dvarn, Ironmaw | Meridian, Tessara |
| Metals | Refined processed materials. | Korrath Prime, Dvarn | Haven, Lirien |
| Tech | Components and electronics. | Korrath Prime, Meridian | Ironmaw, Windhollow, Fallow |
| Luxury | Art, textiles, cultural goods. | Tessara, Lirien | Ironmaw, Dvarn, Char |

Prices fluctuate within a range around each planet’s base price. Standard trade margins run 15–30% on well-planned routes. The best margins come from the most dangerous cross-zone routes.

The shop interface shows buy/sell prices color-coded: green if the planet is cheap for that good, red if expensive, white if average. The player quickly learns to read the economic landscape.

### **Cargo as Food**

Food purchased for trade and food purchased to feed the crew share cargo space. The player must balance profitable cargo against crew sustenance. Food depletes daily based on crew size and species composition (Vellani eat 0.7x, Krellvani eat 1.3x, others eat 1.0x per crew member per day). Running out of food is a morale crisis.

### **Economy Balance Framework**

Crew costs (food \+ pay split) should consume 20–40% of mission income for a well-managed crew. A poorly managed large crew eats 60%+ of income. The credit win target should require efficient Frigate-class operation for 60–90 minutes. Exact values require playtesting — start with a baseline and tune.

*Suggested starting values: Mission rewards 50–300 credits depending on difficulty. Food costs \~2 credits per crew-unit per day. Fuel costs \~5 credits per jump for Skiff, \~10 for Corvette, \~20 for Frigate. Win target: \~25,000 credits.*

## **Mission System**

### **Procedural Generation**

Missions are assembled from modular components. Each mission has a type, destination, difficulty tier, reward, and zero or more complications. The generator weights components by the player’s level, location, and crew composition.

| Component | Options | Purpose |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Type | Cargo Delivery, Passenger Transport, Trade Run, Survey/Scan, Retrieval, Escort, Patrol, Distress Signal | Determines primary activity and which crew roles are exercised. |
| Destination | Any connected planet (or multi-leg: A→B→C) | Defines travel route and associated risks. |
| Difficulty | 1–5 stars, scaling with player level | Sets stat check thresholds and reward multiplier. |
| Reward | Base credits × difficulty multiplier | Economic incentive. |
| Complications | Pirate ambush, customs inspection, environmental hazard, equipment failure, stowaway, distress detour, cargo spoilage | Random modifiers that create crew-relevant challenges. |

Mission boards show 3–5 missions at a time, refreshing each time the player visits. The player can hold 2–3 active missions simultaneously. Each mission type exercises different crew roles:

| Mission Type | Primary Roles Tested | Secondary Roles |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Cargo Delivery | Navigator, Engineer | Security (if complications) |
| Passenger Transport | Comms Officer, Medic | Navigator |
| Trade Run | Navigator, Comms Officer | Engineer |
| Survey/Scan | Science Officer, Navigator | Engineer |
| Retrieval | Security Chief, Engineer | Science Officer |
| Escort | Gunner, Navigator | Comms Officer |
| Patrol | Gunner, Security Chief | Navigator, Science Officer |
| Distress Signal | Medic, Engineer | Gunner (if hostile) |

### **Challenge Resolution**

All challenges resolve through stat checks. The player chooses an approach (Fight, Evade, Negotiate, Investigate, etc. — options vary by situation). Each approach maps to one or two crew roles. The relevant crew member’s effective performance is checked against the difficulty.

**Resolution formula:** Effective Stat \+ Random(0 to Effective Stat) vs. Difficulty Threshold. Effective Stat \= Base Skill × Morale Modifier (0.5–1.2) × Fatigue Modifier (0.4–1.0).

If no crew member covers the required role, the captain attempts it at their generalist stats. If the captain is already handling another simultaneous task (solo play), an additional penalty applies.

### **Outcome Degrees**

| Result | Threshold | Consequence |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Critical Success | Roll \> 2× Difficulty | Clean win, bonus reward, potential positive trait trigger. |
| Success | Roll \> Difficulty | Mission objective met, standard reward. |
| Marginal Success | Roll \> 0.75× Difficulty | Objective met with costs: hull damage, crew fatigue, minor injury. |
| Failure | Roll \> 0.5× Difficulty | Objective failed partially. Significant damage, cargo loss, crew injury. |
| Critical Failure | Roll ≤ 0.5× Difficulty | Severe consequences. Major damage, serious crew injury, possible crew death in extreme cases. |

Critical successes and failures trigger Tier 3 formative events for the involved crew members.

### **Player Choice in Encounters**

When a challenge occurs, the player sees 2–4 approach options. Each shows a rough success probability as a word: “Good odds,” “Risky,” “Desperate.” These are calculated from the relevant crew’s effective stats but never shown as exact numbers. The player makes an informed gamble, not an optimization calculation.

*Example — Pirate Attack: Fight (Gunner \+ Security) “Good odds” / Evade (Navigator \+ Engineer) “Risky” / Negotiate (Comms Officer) “Desperate”. The probabilities shift based on who’s actually on the crew.*

## **Travel System**

Turn-based at the jump level. Each jump between planets is a discrete unit. Per jump: food depletes, fuel depletes, fatigue ticks, crew simulation advances (morale, relationships), and a random encounter check fires based on route danger level.

Between jumps the player sees a brief atmospheric line and taps to continue. Quiet jumps resolve quickly. If an encounter fires, it interrupts with the full challenge resolution flow. Travel rhythm: tap → read a line → tap → read a line, punctuated by occasional meaningful encounters.

### **Random Encounter Types**

| Category | Examples | Frequency Weighting |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Threats | Pirate ambush, asteroid field, patrol inspection, hostile scan | Higher on dangerous routes (High danger: \~40% per jump) |
| Opportunities | Derelict ship with salvage, trader with rare goods, distress beacon (rescue \= potential crew recruit) | Moderate everywhere (\~15% per jump) |
| Crew Events | Interpersonal conflict, morale moment, rest event, discovery | Constant low probability (\~10% per jump, scales with crew size) |
| Nothing | Quiet transit, atmospheric description only | Fills remainder of probability space |

## **Recruitment System**

Available at recruitment stations (5 planets). Each station shows 3–6 procedurally generated candidates. Candidates are weighted by planet species (Gorvian-heavy at Korrath Prime, diverse at Lirien) and quality tier influenced by the player’s level, pay split, and ship reputation.

### **Candidate Profile Display**

Each profile card shows: name, species (with color/icon), role, five stat bars, and a one-line personality descriptor. Tapping expands to show: species traits and vulnerability, and a compatibility note based on current crew (“Your Gorvian crew may clash with this Krellvani recruit”).

### **Offer & Acceptance**

The player’s current pay split determines the offer. Acceptance probability is influenced by: pay split generosity, ship reputation (derived from ship memories and legacy), captain level, and randomness. Better candidates are pickier. Results: Accept, Accept Reluctantly (joins with morale penalty), or Decline.

### **Non-Station Recruitment**

Occasionally, crew candidates appear through missions and encounters: a rescued pilot from a distress signal, a defector from a hostile faction, a stowaway discovered during travel. These bypass recruitment stations and may have unique traits or backgrounds.

## **Crew Dismissal**

The player can dismiss any crew member while docked at any port. The ship view shows a “Dismiss from crew” option on the crew member’s profile. Confirmation shows: their service duration, a warning about morale impact on remaining crew, and the dismissal is final — they don’t return to any recruitment pool. Dismissed crew who served long enough trigger the Tier 4 legacy system.

# **Crew System — All Tiers**

The full crew system as defined in the Crew System GDD applies. This section summarizes each tier’s implementation requirements for the prototype. Refer to the Crew System GDD for complete design rationale.

## **Tier 0 — Mechanical Skeleton**

**Eight roles:** Gunner, Engineer, Navigator, Medic, Comms Officer, Science Officer, Security Chief, Generalist. Each crew member arrives pre-roled.

**Five stats:** Stamina, Cognition, Reflexes, Social, Resourcefulness. Range 0–100. Stats are generated per species aptitude bias with random variation.

**Capability model:** Filled role \= autonomous operation at crew member’s skill. Empty role \= captain handles it manually, one task at a time.

**Pinch-hitting:** Crew can cover other roles at 30–40% effectiveness automatically. Generalists cover at 60–70%.

## **Tier 1 — Living Crew**

**Morale:** 0–100 per crew member. Performance multiplier 0.5–1.2. Ship-wide morale is weighted average.

**Pay split:** One slider: 60/40 captain, 50/50 equitable, or 40/60 crew-favoring. Set once, affects recruitment quality and crew morale.

**Food:** Consumed daily based on crew size and species modifiers. Buy at shop. Quality varies by planet and species match.

**Rest:** Automatic. Fatigue accumulates during active operations, recovers during quiet travel. Modifier 0.4–1.0.

**Safety:** Background calculation. Never shown as number. Emerges from hull state, combat frequency, crew coverage.

**Purpose:** Automatic. Tracks role utilization. Bored crew lose morale.

**Relationships:** Pairwise −100 to \+100. Shift through shared experience and personality compatibility.

**Events:** 80% background flavor, 10% mild nudges, 10% decision points. Appear in message log.

**Performance formula:** Effective \= Base × Morale (0.5–1.2) × Fatigue (0.4–1.0).

## **Tier 2 — Species & Faction Identity**

**Species traits:** Aptitude bias, environmental trait, vulnerability — per the four species defined above.

**Faction access:** None \= outsider, one crew \= baseline, multiple or Comms Officer \= insider. Affects prices, access, missions.

**Cultural friction/synergy:** Starting relationship modifiers per species pairing. Visible at recruitment.

**Species food:** Buying food at a species-appropriate station gives that species a morale boost.

## **Tier 3 — History & Growth**

**Skill progression:** Crew improve through practice. Steep early, flattening curve. Stats approach and can slightly exceed (5–10%) their natural cap.

**Event memory:** 5–8 formative memories per crew member. Trigger \+ Emotional Tag \+ Behavioral Modifier. Same event processed differently by different crew.

**Trait acquisition:** Permanent binary traits from significant events. Every negative trait has a compensating edge and vice versa.

**Ship memories:** Crew-wide modifiers from major events. Affect all current crew and shape crew culture for newcomers.

## **Tier 4 — Deep Dynamics**

**Romance:** Emerges from relationship system. Probability window above \+70 compatibility. Synergy bonuses, linked morale, grief on loss.

**Loyalty:** Hidden value, distinct from morale. Moves slowly. High loyalty \= subtle performance benefits. Low loyalty \= departure arc.

**Injury/disease depth:** Location-specific injuries, species-specific diseases, outbreak scenarios. Medic role becomes critical.

**Crew-generated missions:** High-loyalty veterans surface personal requests. \~1 per 30–40 minutes of late-game play. Optional, rewarding.

**Legacy:** Departed crew leave permanent marks on the ship. Retirement, death, and bitter departure each produce different legacy effects.

**Crew death:** Possible in extreme circumstances (critical failures in high-danger encounters). Rare but impactful. Triggers grief states and legacy.

# **User Interface**

## **Screen Flow**

Main Menu → New Game (name, debug options) or Load Game → Planet View (primary hub). From Planet View: Mission Board, Shop, Recruitment, Shipyard, Ship View, or Depart. Depart → Node Map → Travel View. Encounters interrupt travel with Challenge View. Ship View accessible from any screen.

## **Main Menu**

Title, New Game button, Load Game button (grayed if no save), credits/version. Clean and minimal.

## **New Game Screen**

Captain name selection (short prepared list plus custom text input). Brief intro text. Debug panel (collapsible): start with specific ship class, set starting credits, set starting level, pre-built crew toggle, tier enable/disable checkboxes, time acceleration slider.

## **Planet View**

The primary hub screen. Layout divided into left (informational) and right (interactive) halves.

### **Left Panel — Information**

* Planet shader square (upper-left, prominent). Shader parameters vary by planet type.

* Planet name and faction indicator below the shader square.

* Player panel: Captain name, level, XP bar, credits.

* Ship panel: Ship name/class, hull bar, fuel bar, cargo (used/total), food (days remaining), crew count (current/max).

### **Right Panel — Interaction**

* Vertical stack of contextual buttons based on available services: Mission Board, Shop, Recruitment Station, Shipyard, Depart.

* Tapping a service replaces the button stack with that system’s interface. Back button returns to main planet menu.

* Message log at bottom of right panel: scrolling text area for atmospheric snippets, crew events, notifications. Most crew system narrative output appears here.

## **Ship View**

Accessible from any screen via persistent tab/button.

* Crew slot grid showing filled and empty slots. Each filled slot displays: name, species icon/color, role, status indicator (green/yellow/red).

* Tapping a crew member opens full profile: stat bars, morale, fatigue, role, species traits, relationship summary (small icons with green/red indicators for crewmates), formative memories list, acquired traits.

* Dismiss option available when docked.

* Ship status section: hull, fuel, cargo manifest, food, ship memories.

## **Mission Board**

* List of 3–5 mission cards. Each shows: procedurally generated title, destination, difficulty (1–5 stars), reward, one-line description.

* Tap to expand: full description with atmospheric text, route required, travel time, crew roles tested, special risks.

* Accept button adds to active missions (max 2–3). Active missions shown in a sidebar or tab.

## **Shop**

* Split view: left column \= cargo hold contents with sell prices, right column \= planet goods with buy prices.

* Prices color-coded: green \= cheap here, red \= expensive, white \= average.

* Below trade goods: fuel refill, food restock, ship repair options.

* Tap to buy/sell with quantity selector.

## **Recruitment Station**

* 3–6 candidate profile cards: name, species icon, role, stat bars, personality one-liner.

* Tap to expand: full stats, species traits, compatibility note with current crew.

* Recruit button → offer based on pay split → accept/reluctant/decline result.

## **Shipyard**

* Available ships with specs: crew capacity, cargo, fuel, hull, price. Greyed if level too low or insufficient credits.

* Purchase confirmation with atmospheric text.

## **Node Map (Depart)**

* Full overlay showing node graph. Current planet highlighted. Connected planets shown with route lines.

* Each route displays: jump count, danger indicator (color-coded), fuel cost estimate.

* Visited planets show name \+ faction color. Unvisited show name only.

* Tap destination → confirmation → enter travel.

## **Travel View**

* Jump-by-jump progression. Each jump shows atmospheric text line and a Continue button (or brief auto-advance).

* Crew simulation ticks visible in a message feed.

* Encounters interrupt with full challenge overlay.

## **Challenge View (Encounters & Mission Events)**

* Atmospheric description of situation at top.

* 2–4 approach options, each labeled with relevant crew roles and rough success probability (“Good odds” / “Risky” / “Desperate”).

* Player picks approach. Resolution plays out in 2–3 lines of prose.

* Results display: credits/loot, hull changes, crew status changes, events triggered.

## **Win Screen**

* Triggered when credits reach target. Summary: total jumps, missions completed, crew who served (including departed), ship memories, current roster with full history. The player’s story, told through system-generated data.

* Continue playing option (no new content, crew sim keeps running) or return to main menu.

# **Development Phases**

The prototype is built in six phases. Each phase produces a playable build that can be tested before the next phase begins. Dependencies are listed explicitly — no phase should begin until its dependencies are complete and tested.

Build the world and the basic gameplay loop with no crew. The player flies solo, takes missions, trades goods, levels up, and saves progress. This phase validates the economic model, travel system, and mission framework before crew complexity is added.

None. This is the base layer.

* Event text templates with procedural fill (crew name, species, role, location)

**PHASE 4  Crew Tier 3 — History, Growth & Traits**

Add the memory and growth systems that make each crew member a unique product of the player’s journey. This phase transforms the crew from functional characters into people shaped by experience.

### **Dependencies**

Phase 3 complete and tested. Crew events and encounters producing varied outcomes reliably.

### **Deliverables**

**4.1 Skill Progression**

* Role experience tracking: accumulates when crew member’s role is exercised

* Effective stat grows from starting value (below cap) toward natural cap. Steep early, flattening curve.

* Exceptional performance can push 5–10% beyond cap

* Pinch-hit experience: slower growth in non-primary role when covering

* Ship View profile shows growth progress (current effective vs. cap)

**4.2 Event Memory System**

* Formative memory data structure: trigger, emotional\_tag, behavioral\_modifier

* Memory generation on significant events (critical success/failure, combat survival, crew conflict)

* Emotional tag determination based on crew member stats and current morale

* Behavioral modifier application: adjusts performance in matching future situations

* Memory cap: 5–8 per crew member, newer significant events push out older minor ones

* Memory display on crew profile

* Memory-referenced dialogue: event text occasionally references a crew member’s past (“Rask tenses up — last time she saw pirates this close, it didn’t end well”)

**4.3 Trait Acquisition**

* Trait data model: name, description, positive\_effect, negative\_effect, acquisition\_condition

* Trait check system: evaluates conditions after significant events

* Positive traits implemented: Battle-Tested, Spacer’s Instinct, Trusted by \[Faction\], plus 3–4 more

* Negative traits implemented: Scarred, Grudge-Bearer, Haunted, plus 3–4 more

* Every negative trait has compensating edge, every positive trait has subtle cost

* Trait acquisition notification as crew event

* Traits visible on crew profile

**4.4 Ship Memories**

* Ship memory data model: event\_description, modifier\_type, modifier\_value, date\_acquired

* Ship memory generation from major events (catastrophic battle, extraordinary discovery, faction betrayal)

* Ship-wide modifier application to all current crew

* New crew affected by existing ship culture through relationship system

* Ship memories displayed in Ship View

### **Assets Checklist**

* Trait descriptions: \~15 trait definitions with flavor text

* Memory-referenced event text templates: \~20 lines that reference specific memory types

* Trait acquisition notification text: \~15 announcement templates

* Ship memory event text: \~10 major event templates

### **Playtesting Focus**

Do veterans feel different from fresh recruits? Is skill growth noticeable but not exploitable through grinding? Do memories make crew feel like individuals? Are traits fair (compensating edges feel real)? Does losing a veteran feel meaningfully worse than losing a new hire? Are ship memories creating a sense of shared history?

**PHASE 5  Crew Tier 4 — Deep Dynamics**

Add the emotional depth layer: romance, loyalty arcs, medical complexity, crew-generated missions, and legacy. This is the system that turns a crew into a family.

### **Dependencies**

Phase 4 complete and tested. Event memory and trait systems generating varied crew histories reliably.

### **Deliverables**

**5.1 Romance System**

* Romance probability check: fires when pairwise relationship exceeds \+70 with compatible personality profiles

* Probability: \~5% per event cycle above \+70, increasing with higher relationship

* Couple effects: synergy bonus (coordinated performance), linked morale, enhanced pinch-hit willingness

* Couple vulnerabilities: partner injury morale hit, death grief state, breakup fallout

* Grief state: deep morale collapse, erratic performance. Resolves over time into Resolved or Broken trait.

* Breakup mechanics: relationship drops below threshold under sustained stress. Both take morale hit, crew takes sides.

* Romance events in message log: formation, milestones, tension, breakup

**5.2 Loyalty System**

* Hidden loyalty value per crew member: 0–100, distinct from morale

* Loyalty growth triggers: competent captaincy, shared hardship with good outcomes, value-aligned decisions

* Per-crew value preferences (discoverable through play): some value caution, some valor, some exploration

* High loyalty effects: volunteer for danger, slight above-cap performance in crisis, buffer morale dips for others

* Low loyalty departure arc: withdrawal → vocal unhappiness → ultimatum → departure

* Ultimatum system: crew member demands change (more money, less danger, specific crewmate gone) or leaves next port

* No forced retention — player can meet demand, negotiate, or accept departure

**5.3 Injury & Disease Depth**

* Injury location system: injuries specify affected area, impacting different roles differently

* Severity levels: minor (quick recovery), moderate (extended recovery, temp stat reduction), severe (permanent impairment, trait trigger)

* Species-specific diseases: certain environments/cargo trigger species-targeted pathogens

* Disease spread: compatible species can catch from each other (quarantine decisions)

* Medic role amplified: skilled medic reduces severity, speeds recovery, catches disease early

* Hospital visits: expensive but handle severe cases the medic cannot

**5.4 Crew-Generated Missions**

* Generation trigger: high loyalty \+ sufficient history (memories and traits) \+ time threshold

* Mission content derived from crew member’s species, faction, memories, relationships

* Examples implemented: homeworld visit, confrontation with past, closure at trauma site, shared adventure with friend

* Rewards: loyalty gain, trait resolution (Haunted → At Peace), unique items, story moment

* Optional: declining causes small loyalty dip, not crisis

* Frequency: \~1 per 30–40 minutes of late-game play

**5.5 Legacy System**

* Legacy trigger on crew departure (dismissal, voluntary departure, retirement, death)

* Legacy type determined by departure circumstances and service duration

* Positive legacy: efficiency bonus, morale bonus, skill bonus for future crew in same role

* Negative legacy: suspicion, morale penalty, cautionary effect

* Death legacy: memorial effect (combat morale resistance)

* Legacies displayed in Ship View as ship history

* Legacies persist for remainder of playthrough

### **Assets Checklist**

* Romance event text templates: \~15 (formation, milestones, tension, breakup, grief)

* Loyalty departure arc text: \~10 withdrawal/vocal/ultimatum templates

* Disease/injury text: \~15 medical event templates

* Crew-generated mission templates: \~10 mission narrative frameworks

* Legacy effect descriptions: \~10 legacy flavor texts

* Value-preference hint text: \~20 subtle dialogue lines revealing crew values

### **Playtesting Focus**

Does romance feel organic, not forced? Are loyalty-based departures emotionally resonant? Do medical crises create tense non-combat drama? Are crew-generated missions worth doing? Does legacy make departed crew feel remembered? Is the overall system generating stories you want to tell someone about?
